Tools for Data Journos

Code4SA's curated list of tools for data journalists for writing, finding and investigating stories.

Wazimap.co.za

Detailed demographics and election results for everywhere in South Africa, right down to the ward level. Age, income, household goods, employment, toilets, water and other service delivery details across the country from the 2011 Census.

OpenByLaws.org.za

Full local by-laws for Cape Town and Johannesburg, with more on the way. Easy to search, read and share.

Parliamentary Monitoring Group - PMG.org.za

Everything from the day-to-day workings of parliament, including committee memberships, meeting reports, hansards and questions and replies.

Things we love

Useful Tools

Google Sheets's IMPORTHTML function

This function for Google Sheets can grab a table off of a website and drop it directly into your spreadsheet. Awesome.

Import.io

Import.io makes it super-easy to scrape (some) websites. You can use the Import.io website, or download the desktop tool for more power.

Tabula tabula.technology

This great tool sucks tables out of PDFs, provided they’re not too big.

sourceAFRICA sourceafrica.net

An instance of DocumentCloud, this tool will let you deal with large stacks of badly-scanned documents — exactly the type that government likes to foist on its media in response to PAIA requests.

Open Refine openrefine.org

No matter what you call it, it’s our go-to tool for cleaning dirty data. This tool will save you weeks of work.
