News Editor

We are launching Africa’s first data journalism school and are looking for an experienced digital-savvy news editor to run an onsite newsroom. Starting in February 2016, the school will run regular three month programmes of intensive coursework, as well as an active data-driven newsroom.

In addition to training, the school will be a centre for news innovation. Through the newsroom, we will experiment with different approaches to delivering content to audiences with an emphasis on actionable news. We are actively involved in ongoing projects that promote innovation in the media space through the use of data-driven storytelling and the school will play an important role in achieving this.

You'll play a key role as leader of our newsroom team and be expected to help drive this process. The successful applicant will be involved in the following:

  • Share your knowledge and experience gained working in a digital newsroom, producing cross-platform content that includes compelling visualisations
  • Help Code for South Africa establish a data-driven, digital newsroom that works across multiple platforms
  • Assume the role of overseeing the day-to-day editorial management
  • Compile and oversee a news diary with short, medium and long term data-driven stories/news projects, working closely with data wranglers, coders, analysts and students
  • Commit to a schedule to publish work in the local, national and international press
  • Participate in the teaching of short modules in our data journalism school
  • Provide journalistic insight and your eye for a good story, working with students and the technical team
  • Work with students to explore datasets for potential stories and help decide on the way these will be covered
  • Help pioneer new approaches to telling stories, especially in a South African context. This will include exploring new ways of personalising content, as well as delivery of content to low-powered mobile devices
  • Participate in exploring new funding models for stories
  • You may be involved in other Code for South Africa projects which relate to open data

To apply, contact jobs@code4sa.org.

Community Manager

Looking for a way to make a meaningful contribution to society and work towards social change? Are you passionate about building communities and connecting like-minded people? If so, why not chat to us?

We are looking for a vibrant community manager to join our growing team. An independent all-rounder, the ideal candidate will be comfortable formulating strategy to build and maintain civic tech communities as well as implement this strategy. This role has scope to grow with the organisation and we would prefer to place someone who enjoys the idea of growing with us.

Role and Responsibilities

  • Content creation – writing blog posts, articles, newsletters, communications materials, and material for social media channels
  • Social media marketing – creating, managing and growing the community’s presence through blogs, Twitter, Facebook, and other relevant online tools
  • Events and event planning – attending and planning industry events and meetups for the community
  • Analytics – Leverage market analysis, competitive research and customer insights to define our community platform roadmap and continually find ways to improve through testing and new initiatives
  • Community strategy – plan and execute community strategy and member programs to grow the membership, drive engagement, and promote meaningful member-to-member interactions
  • Communications – act as primary point of contact for day-to-day operations of the support community
  • Campaigns – Conceptualise and run community campaigns
  • Budgeting – manage annual budget

Requirements

  • Passion for civic technology and data
  • Outgoing personality
  • Excellent communication skills, both written and verbal
  • Proficient with Google docs
  • Social media genius
  • Great sense of humour
  • Organisational skills
  • Adaptability
  • Previous community management experience would be nice, but is not essential
  • Interest in gamification and behavioural economics is a bonus

Start Date: To be confirmed
Salary: In line with experience and market related
Location: Cape Town

To apply, contact jobs@code4sa.org.

Newsroom Developer

We are launching Africa’s first data journalism school and are looking for a developer for the school and our data-driven newsroom. The developer will support the students and newsroom by building interactive tools and visualisations, wrangling data, and diving into analytics.

Everything we do is open source and often draws public attention. You’ll get to work on a wide variety of projects which keeps it interesting and means there’s lots to learn. We’re a small team so you can get your hands dirty in almost everything we do.

Requirements

  • Good knowledge of HTML, Javascript, CSS, D3, Bootstrap and jQuery
  • Proficiency in Python is a plus
  • Hands-on experience with data processing
  • Able to develop data visualisations
  • Able to work independently

Start Date: January 2015
Salary: In line with experience and market related
Location: Cape Town

To apply, contact jobs@code4sa.org.

About Code for South Africa

Code for South Africa is a non-profit civic technology lab. We use data and technology to promote informed decision-making for social good. Our base of operations is Codebridge in Claremont where we are helping to create a civic technology movement.
